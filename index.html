International Medical Missions

This past March I was blessed with the opportunity to fly down to Nicaragua with a few classmates and several Nurse Practitioners. We met an incredible doctor down there named Ruben Lopez who had all of the connections and transportation that we needed.

Our team set up clinics in small villages outside of Managua. There we assessed, diagnosed, proscribed medicine, educated, and help teach about the Gospel of Christ. Each village we went to was more eye opening than the last; more and more families would come with such generosity and love toward us at the clinics. After a few days of clinics in the villages our group visited a children's hospital, specifically a cancer unit, where we gave out gifts and prayed with kids and their families. Many of the doctors and nurses at the hospital worked 24 hour shifts with little break time in between and were ecstatic that we could come play and comfort the families for a short time.

The people in Nicaragua left a huge impact in my worldview as well as my heart. They are some of the most loving, caring, and compassionate people I have ever had the pleasure to meet.
